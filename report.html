1  Introduction

Recent work in this field is shifting from early proof-of-concept demonstrations toward studies that ask for whom, under what conditions, and at what cost an intervention works. Research attention has visibly expanded into adaptive, data-driven decision-making and tighter coupling with the systems where the work actually happens1.

The evidence base, however, is uneven. Some outcomes are now supported by repeated randomized trials and meta-analyses, while others rest on a handful of small or short-horizon studies2. This report maps where the evidence is strong, where it is thin, and where the field is heading next.

3.2  Frontier directions

The first frontier is adaptive, individualized decision-making, where models adjust intervention timing and intensity from real-time context3. The second is privacy-preserving, on-device computation, which trades raw performance for data locality and trust4. The third is workflow augmentation — moving from a standalone tool toward something embedded in everyday practice, with measurable time savings reported5.

4  Discussion

The strongest results cluster around outcomes that are concrete, measurable, and trackable over short horizons. As tasks require more sustained behaviour change or deeper integration, the evidence thins and implementation difficulty rises6.

5  Conclusion

The frontier is clear, but success is decided less by the core method than by the implementation questions around it — durability, interoperability, trust, and equitable access. The most valuable near-term opportunities remain the high-frequency, standardized tasks that fit cleanly into existing workflows7.

Open research questions

Sustained retention directly bounds achievable effect sizes and the value of any adaptive model; current studies rarely follow users long enough to answer it.
Access, adoption, and adherence gaps recur across the corpus, but disaggregated reporting is rare — making the mechanisms hard to target.
Interoperability standards, governance, and trust appear to be the binding constraint on turning efficacy into scaled, dependable service delivery.
